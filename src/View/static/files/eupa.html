Minimum Qualifications/Eligibility

Participation in MOSES shall be open to military and government agencies along with interested parties from the non-military community including academic and industrial enterprises. Applicants must demonstrate:

·        a relevant and serious use case for a virtual world application

·        a capability to function at a basic level within the environment

·        a contribution to open simulator development and military training applications

Roles and Responsibilities

Participants shall be categorized by one or more of three roles with related responsibilities:

Participants

Participants are those individuals who have been granted a basic MOSES account. A participant is someone who has made the case for admittance by explaining how they can contribute to MOSES. They shall accept and meet the following requirements:

·        abide by the MOSES Distribution Agreement.

·        behave according to the acceptable use/behavior policy as defined under Terms of Release / Termination.

·        be an adult of 18 years or older.

·        conduct themselves in a professional manner at all times.

·        shall respect the dress code which means they shall remain clothed at all times as if they were in an office or other professional setting.

·        create their account using their legal name.

·        comply with any other community standards for MOSES as implemented by the MOSES Management.

·        They shall pursue projects of general interest to military uses of virtual world technologies.

·        if publishing and using the MOSES name/logo/technology, they will inform MOSES management.

·        not act as an official representative of the MOSES project in any official capacity

·        MOSES Management reserves the right to request PAO authorization from other commands for any material that may be questionably sensitive. Participant from a sister service or lab uploads should acquire PAO approval at the Distribution A level for their work before uploading it to MOSES. MOSES may be considered to be a virtual parallel public venue as a conference.

Estate Managers

Participants who wish to elevate their work in MOSES by being granted access to simulator resources (Estate) will be required to submit a project proposal. Estate managers are participants who have been given the responsibility of managing an estate (simulator resources) in MOSES. In addition to the requirements of Participants, they shall accept and meet the following requirements:

·         Estate managers shall have a minimum level of proficiency that demonstrates their ability to manage a simulated environment. This information should be included in the estate project proposal.

·         They shall be skilled at project management

·         They shall be willing to use their skills to assist in mentoring or providing training for the community

·         Estate managers shall also be expected to work within the MOSES environment to learn and perform studies of military relevance

·         Estate managers will be held responsible for all the content and activities on their simulators

·         They are encouraged to use content licensed under a creative commons license

·         All Estates will submit a project summary associated with the research activities on the simulator.

·         Estate Managers shall be expected to submit to the MOSES management a project summary and quarterly reports regarding their progress. This shall be in addition to updating their project status quarterly to MOSES management.

·         They shall be encouraged to participate in an orientation covering estate management skills

·         They are to notify ARL/STTC of exiting participants so they can be removed as a participant. This request can be included in the quarterly reports if no immediate security issues exist.

MOSES Management

MOSES Management hosts the MOSES environment at the US ARL-HRED-STTC Orlando site. The MOSES Management role is limited to STTC personnel at this time. MOSES Management is responsible for the following:

·        Admitting new users and creating estates/assign Estate Managers

·        MOSES technology management and backup (server resources, networking resources, storage)

·        MOSES management shall approve any training or educational material placed in public areas

·        MOSES management/STTC personnel reserve the right to inspect the data collected and housed on the simulators at any time.

Linkages to Outside Servers

Participants are not automatically granted permission to allow objects in MOSES to poll or report to an outside database. The most common request for this data exchange activity is to connect MOSES to an external artificial intelligence engine.

This exchange of data into and out of MOSES will only be allowed if permission is first obtained from MOSES management. In the event the US ARL-HRED-STTC information assurance personnel determine the proposed data exchange activities are not aligned with DoD computing or security policy, permission will not be granted to perform the data exchange activities. Any attempt to perform the unauthorized data exchange activity will result in immediate termination of participation and suspension of all accounts related to your activities and organization.

Requests will be considered on a case-by-case basis to ensure security issues can be determined from the specific requirements. Circumventing security measures or exploiting a vulnerability in the Open Simulator software or MOSES system is grounds for immediate dismissal from the MOSES project and may result in civil and criminal actions.

Terms of Participation

The MOSES grid is a technology experiment and not a service or implied to be a service. The domain covered by this agreement is the MOSES grid operated by the STTC. Therefore, participation will not be limited to a specific length of time, and participants shall understand that MOSES may be closed to participation at any time. In addition, an account may be removed at any time for any reason, subject to STTC decision.

This agreement does not cover any use of content created by participants in MOSES nor the use of any resources released under an open source license by MOSES.

Terms of Release/Termination

Participation can be terminated at any time for not abiding by any of the following requirements:

·        Participation is a privilege and may be terminated at any time.

·        Participants will not post or transmit prohibited content, including any content that is illegal, harassing, or violates any person's intellectual property rights

·        Participants agree that they will not post or transmit content or code that may be harmful, impede other users' functionality, invade other users' privacy, or surreptitiously or negatively impact any system or network.

·        Participants agree that they will not upload or use any material for which they do not own the copyright or for which they do not have the permission to use.

·        Participants agree they will not unlawfully impersonate another person or another person's avatar. Participants are not to share passwords.

·        Participants agree they will not post, depict, display, create, transfer anything related to or containing spam, pornography, gambling, discriminatory, offensive content.

·        Participants agree they will not exhibit offensive and/or unlawful behavior. For example, aggression, defamation, discrimination of a person's or avatar's sex, religion, race, age, or sexual orientation.

·        REPEAT: Circumventing security measures or exploiting a vulnerability in the Open Simulator software or MOSES system is grounds for immediate dismissal from the MOSES project and may result in civil and criminal actions.
